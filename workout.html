🏋️ Coach's Note — Push Day With 4 days you can drop the calf raises here — they move to Legs. The dumbbell fly and DB lateral raises are serious upgrades over barbell pullovers and KBs: more range of motion, unilateral correction, and better isolation. On the OHP, add weight when you hit the top of your rep range for 2 consecutive sessions (double progression). Same rule for bench.

Forearm extensors and grip. Go light.
Meadows Row
Added3×8–10 each
One end of the barbell in a corner (landmine style), straddle it and row with one arm. Hits the upper-outer lat in a way standard rows can't reach. Brace your free hand on your knee.

5 rounds × 40m

Added2–3 × max hold

🏋️ Coach's Note — Pull Day The chest-supported DB row is one of the best back exercises you can add — it completely removes lower back from the equation and lets you hammer the mid-back with full range. Face pulls are non-negotiable for long-term shoulder health. Bicep work moves to Day 4 from this day if the session feels long — keep the EZ curls and hammer curls here for now, they're quick.

Barbell on back, sit on bench, hinge forward at the hip. Isolates the hamstrings and spinal erectors without the lower back fatigue of standing good mornings. Go light — the stretch is the point.

4×15–20
Full range — deep stretch at bottom. Slow eccentric (3 sec down).

🏋️ Coach's Note — Legs Day This is your heaviest session — protect it. If front squats feel like too much after back squats, swap to a Goblet Squat with a DB for the same quad stimulus with less spinal load. The DB upgrade on split squats and lunges is a great move — you'll load more naturally and have better balance. Calf raises moved from Day 1 back to here where they belong.

Oblique lateral flexion. Hold DB in one hand, other hand on head. Don't rotate.
Arms · Biceps
Dumbbell Incline Curl
New · DB3×10–12
Seated on incline bench, arms hang back. Maximises long head stretch of bicep. Slow and controlled only.
Dumbbell Concentration Curl
New · DB3×12 each
Peak contraction and mind-muscle connection. Brace elbow on inner thigh.
Zottman Curl
Added3×10–12
Curl up supinated (palms up), rotate to pronated (palms down) at the top, lower slowly. Bicep on the way up, brachioradialis and forearm on the way down. Two stimuli, one exercise.
Arms · Triceps
Dumbbell Kickbacks
New · DB3×12–15 each
Lateral head isolation. Upper arm parallel to floor — don't let it drop. Squeeze hard at extension.
Dumbbell Tricep Extension (Lying)
New · DB3×10–12
Like skull crushers but with DBs — rotate at the top to pronate. Better elbow comfort for many.
Shoulder Accessory
Dumbbell Arnold Press
New · DB3×10–12
Hits all three delt heads through rotation. Start palms facing you, rotate to face out as you press.
Dumbbell Front Raise
New · DB3×12
Anterior delt isolation. Alternate arms or bilateral. Don't swing — control is everything here.
Grip Work
Plate Pinches or Towel Hangs
2–3 rounds × 30 sec
Grip transfers directly to deadlift and rows. Do this at the end when you're tired — real grip training.
🏋️ Coach's Note — Core & Arms Day This is your new day — the gift of a 4-day split. It frees up Days 1 and 2 to focus purely on strength, and gives your arms and core dedicated volume they weren't getting before. The DB incline curl and concentration curl are two of the best bicep builders — the stretch and peak contraction positions hit different parts of the muscle. Arnold press is a must — it covers all three delt heads in one movement. Keep intensity moderate here since Days 1–3 are the heavy hitters.
